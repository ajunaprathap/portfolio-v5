Choiz is an iOS app designed to help people with food restrictions/dietary preferences find restaurants near them that cater to their diet.

VIEW PROTOTYPE

Problem

Finding restaurants that cater to your dietary restrictions is always difficult since most of the apps that exist today only provide limited filter options such as just vegan or vegetarian and this is not very useful to people who are on other kinds of dietary restrictions. Also, most restaurants that mark themselves as “diet friendly” usually only have salads or bland pasta as an option. With the rise in the number of people identifying themselves with at least one kind of dietary restriction, trying to find restaurants that fit their diet is a major frustration.

Solution

Choiz is an app that can help users explore restaurants near them that have food options that are specific to their diet. All they have to do is select their diet and enter their location information to see a list of restaurants near them that have food options for them. The app will display food from the menu that is specific to the users selected dietary preference, thus skipping the frustrating task of having to skim through the entire menu to find an option. Users can also read reviews, make reservations or have their food delivered or ready for pickup.

Process

User Survey

I started my research with a survey posted online to multiple media platforms, public forums and email contacts. I wanted to understand what frustrations people with diet restrictions faced when searching for food using the current apps. I also wanted to know what it was that they were looking forward to in an app that would help them find food based on their diet.

User Survey Analysis

  • 60.7% participants said that they had some kind of dietary restriction / dietary preference
  • 62.1% participants said that online reviews and rating was the most important thing they considered when choosing which restaurant they wanted to dine in.
  • 82.4% participants said that they checked restaurant menus online before going to any restaurant
  • 52.5% participants said that not being able to identify which restaurants had food options for them was frustrating
  • 70% participants said that they ordered food for delivery/pickup atleast once a month

When asked about their frustrations, they said that most of the restaurant menus did not highlight food items for people with dietary restrictions. Secondly, they were also finding it very difficut to identify which restaurants had food options for them.With this information, I felt confident to move forward with developing the concept of Choiz.

User Personas

At this point though I had a better idea of who my target audience were, I interviewed survey respondents to dig deeper into their responses and clarify my understanding of the problem and my potential solution. So based on this I created three user personas.

VIEW PERSONAS

Competitive Analysis

Since there are many restaurant search engines and apps already in the market, I took a closer look at three of my competitors : Yelp, Happy Cow and Uber Eats to see what their positions were in the market so that I could identify where these apps were falling short for users.

From the SWOT analysis I understood that all three services have their own pros and cons, for example, all of them have a simple and user friendly UI, but Yelp and UberEats don't allow their users to make a selection based on dietary restrictions. Similarly though Happy cow has a friendly logo design and also allows users find food based on their diet, its disadvantage is that its pages are too colorful and distracting and also it provided only limited filter options such as vegetarian, vegan and gluten-free filters. These were opportunities that I saw for my product.

Wireframes And Usability Testing

Low-fidelity wireframes were created, tested and experienced many different iterations before graduating to the the high-fidelity stage. During the first round of usability testing random users were asked to complete a specific set of tasks. Overall, the three users had a positive response to Choiz and were able to navigate the site smoothly.

Test Result and Iterations

1. When users were signing up for an account, they suggested to provide a skip option so that they could go directly to the site without having to do the onboarding process first.

2. For the task where users had to place a food order, they suggested to add an edit option so that they could edit their order easily even at the last minute.

Brand Story

When I got to the branding phase, I wanted this product to not only have a catchy name but to also have a name that's related to my brand's purpose. Since the main purpose of my app was to help people find the right food options easily, I wanted my brand name to easily convey this message to my users. Through proper mind-mapping I decided to settle on the name CHOIZ for my app because this name clearly lets the users know that my app was to going to help them choose food based on their diet.

The next thing I had to decide was my logo. For this I started by initially sketching a few logo ideas on paper. Then I created digital versions of my sketches to get a better feel of the logo before finally settling on a logo design that was perfect for my brand.

Color

I decided to go with green as my primary color mainly because green is a color that promotes the feeling of abundance. It is a color that's also associated with energy, health, freshness, wellness and safety and since my app was mainly concerned with providing food options, I felt that this color was perfect for my brand.

Typography

I decided to use Roboto font for my logo specially because it matchd well with the rest of the typefaces I had selected for my design. For the marketing page, I used Montserrat for the H1 text and Source sans Pro for the body text. For the iOS app I decided to stick with SF pro so that my users could get the feel of Apple.

Logo Design

Before finalizing the logo, I did a preference test to see if my users preferred a logo with a fork or a logo with a spoon for my app. From the preference test resut it was clear that my users preferred the logo with the spoon, so I decided to move ahead with that.

Hi-Fi Mockups And Preference Testing

Now that my color, typography and logo had been finalized, I next started to work on my high fidelity mockups using the Figma tool. I also conducted a quick preference test to decide what button needs to be used when users palce a food order. Apart from this I also checked to see how users wanted the delivery/pickup option to be displayed.

Preference Test Results

Usability Testing and Visual Design

Next I created a prototype which was then used for the second round of usability testing. This time I asked my users to mainly focus on the visual and the UI elements because I wanted to test if my branding was successful or not.

Usability Test Results

Result 1: For the task where users had to rate and write a review, they were very confused. Most users clicked on the stars instead of the link. To reduce this confusion, I removed the stars and made the link clearer.

Result 2: In the restaurant detail screen, users felt that the sub-navbar looked different from the rest of the design, so I improved this section by using icons instead.

Final Design

After updating my design based on the results of the usability testing, I had my final design reviewed by my team members at the Thinkful community. The feedback I received from them, as well as my mentor was invaluable. Also, focusing on what users wanted from Choiz and letting them influence the overall design made Choiz a user friendly tool that is intuitive and impactful.

Takeaways

I think my biggest takeaway from this project was realizing the importance of conducting usability tests as well as preference tests at every stage of my process. This helped me save time as well as helped me improve the design. I also learned how to implement modern practices and techniques to elevate my designs.
